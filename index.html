Getting Started

When you first open GradeSight Pro, you will see the Welcome Screen asking if you are a GWH staff member. Click "Yes, I'm a GWH Teacher".

You will then be asked if you have registered before. New teachers should click "No, I'm new here" and create a username and password. Your username is typically your first and last name combined (e.g. priyasharma). The app will suggest a shorter version like ps@GWH — click it to use it.

Returning teachers click "Yes, I've registered" and log in with their username and password.

After logging in for the first time, you will be taken to the Profile Setup screen. Fill in your name, the subjects you teach, and map each subject to its class sections. For example, if you teach Mathematics to classes 8A and 9B, add "Mathematics" as a subject and then type "8A" and "9B" in the sections box for that subject. If you are a class teacher, enter your class section (e.g. 8A) in the Class Teacher field.

⚠ Important: After setting up your profile, immediately click "Download Backup" at the bottom of the sidebar. Store this file safely in Google Drive or a USB drive. If you ever clear your browser data, you will need this file to recover your account.
📊

Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home screen. It shows a summary of your students, subjects, sections, and upcoming events at a glance.

The stat cards at the top show total students, subjects you teach, assigned sections, and upcoming events. Below them, the class-subject chips show every class-subject pair you teach.

The Countdown widget shows how many days are left until your next upcoming event. The Timetable Widget shows today's schedule at a glance. The Top Scorers card highlights the highest-performing student in each exam for each class.

The Homework Due Soon widget shows upcoming assignments — click "View All" to jump to the full Homework section.

To edit your profile (name, subjects, sections), scroll to the bottom of the sidebar and click "Edit Profile".

🗓

Timetable

Go to the Timetable section from the sidebar. Click "Build Timetable" to start. Choose the number of periods per day using the input at the top, then click "Build Grid".

A grid of days and periods will appear. On the right, you will see colour-coded blocks for each of your subjects and sections. Drag a block from the palette onto any cell in the grid to place it. To move a placed block, drag it to a different cell. Double-click a placed block to remove it.

To rename a block (for example to specify "Mathematics – 8A"), hover over it and click the small pencil icon that appears in the top-right corner of the cell.

When done, click "Save Timetable". The saved timetable appears below with two export buttons: Print opens a printer-friendly view, and Copy as Image copies a high-resolution PNG of the timetable to your clipboard — ideal for sharing with students or posting on a notice board.

📅

Calendar & Events

The Calendar section shows a monthly calendar view. Days that have events display small coloured dots beneath the date number. Click any date to see all events scheduled for that day and to add a new one.

To add an event, click the "+ Add Event" button. Fill in the event name, choose a category (Exam, Holiday, Meeting, Activity, or Other), pick the date and optionally a time, and add any notes. You can also set a recurring rule — weekly or monthly — with an end date, so the event automatically repeats on the calendar.

The dot colour on the calendar matches the category: indigo for Exams, green for Holidays, amber for Meetings, pink for Activities. In the All Events panel below the calendar, you can filter events by category using the pill buttons. Past events appear at reduced opacity.

When an event's time arrives, GradeSight Pro will play an alarm sound and show an overlay notification. Click Dismiss to stop it, or Snooze to be reminded again in 5 minutes.

👩‍🎓

Students

Go to the Students section. The General tab shows all your students as colour-coded cards. Use the Select Class dropdown — it only shows the classes you teach.

To add a student, click "Add Student", enter their name and roll number, and click Add. To add many students at once, click "Import CSV". Download the provided template, fill it in with names and roll numbers, then upload it — or paste the CSV data directly into the text box.

Click any student card to open their Student Profile. This shows their overall average across all exams, their best and weakest subjects, attendance percentage, a performance trend chart, and a full breakdown of every exam's marks. You can also add Notes about a student from their profile — click the notepad icon on their card.

To remove a student, open their profile and click the red "Remove Student" button.

The Focused View panel at the top lets you pin a specific class and subject, so the marks table filters down to just that combination — useful when you teach many classes.

📝

Entering Marks

In the Students section, switch to one of the four exam tabs: Unit Test 1, Term 1, Unit Test 2, or Term 2 (Final). Select a class to load the marks grid.

Click any cell and type the mark. Default maximums: Unit Tests are out of 25, Terms are out of 80. Computer is out of 50 in terms. If a mark exceeds the maximum, the cell turns red and the value is not saved — correct it before moving on.

You can customise the maximum mark or set a subject-specific pass threshold by expanding the ⚙ Custom Max Marks & Pass Thresholds panel above the grid. Grade boundaries (A+, A, B+, etc.) can also be adjusted from the Grade Boundaries panel.

Navigate between cells using Tab, Enter, and the arrow keys. Class teachers can enter marks for all subjects; subject teachers can only enter marks for the subjects they are assigned.

Once marks are entered, the bar below the table shows the Class Topper, Class Average, and subject pass/fail counts. Click "Export CSV" to download the marks, or "Print" to open a clean printable report with grade calculations and rankings.

To prevent accidental edits after finalization, click "Lock Exam". A PIN is set and the marks table becomes read-only until you unlock it with the same PIN.

✅

Attendance

Open the Attendance section from the sidebar. Select a class and you will see each student as a card in Daily mode.

To mark a student Present, click the green ✓ button at the bottom-left of their card — the card fills with a green sweep. To mark Absent, click the red ✗ button at the bottom-right. Clicking the same button again clears the mark. A toast notification confirms each action.

Use the ‹ › arrows or the date picker to navigate between dates. The "All Present" and "All Absent" buttons mark the entire class at once.

Double-click a student card to open their Attendance Summary — showing overall percentage, a monthly bar breakdown, and a custom date range picker. You can also quickly jump to This Month, This Year, or the full Academic Year.

The Monthly Summary tab lists all students sorted by lowest attendance first, making at-risk students easy to spot. You can print an Attendance Warning Letter for any student directly from their card.

To configure Holidays (which are automatically excluded from attendance totals), go to the 🗓 Holidays tab. Saturdays and Sundays are holidays by default. You can add named date ranges (e.g. Summer Break: April 1 – June 2), individual dates (e.g. Diwali), or recurring weekly off-days (e.g. every Friday).

📋

Homework & Assignments

The Homework section lets you track assignments across all your classes in one place. Open it from the sidebar.

To add an assignment, click "+ Add Assignment". Fill in the title, select the class and subject, set a due date, and optionally add a description. Click Save — the assignment appears in the list immediately.

Each assignment card shows its title, class, subject, due date, and current status. Assignments are automatically labelled Pending, Overdue (if the due date has passed), or Done. Click the ✓ Mark Done button on a card to mark it as completed.

Use the Class and Status filter dropdowns at the top to narrow the list — for example, show only Overdue assignments for a specific class.

The Dashboard also shows a Homework Due Soon widget with the next few upcoming assignments so you never miss a deadline.

📈

Analytics

The Analytics section gives you a bird's-eye view of performance across your entire teaching load. Open it from the sidebar.

The Section Comparison tab lets you pick an exam and compare the average marks of every class side by side. Each class is shown as a bar with the class average percentage, making it easy to see which sections are performing well and which need attention.

The Subject Heatmap tab shows a colour-coded grid — rows are your classes, columns are subjects — where each cell's colour reflects the average score for that class in that subject. Green means strong, red means weak, grey means no data. This is a quick way to identify subjects that need intervention across multiple classes.

Use the exam dropdown at the top to switch between Unit Test 1, Term 1, Unit Test 2, and Term 2 data. Analytics only reflect marks that have already been entered.

🧮

Calculators

The Calculators section has three tools, selectable via the tabs at the top.

Math — a full scientific calculator supporting trigonometric functions (sin, cos, tan with Degree/Radian toggle and INV for inverse functions), logarithms, roots, powers, factorial, absolute value, constants (π and e), and memory (MC, MR, MS, M+, M−). You can type expressions with parentheses and press = or Enter to evaluate. A calculation history panel on the right lets you recall previous results.

Score — enter a student's raw marks and the maximum marks for each subject to instantly calculate percentage, grade, and overall average. Useful for quick manual checks.

The Percentile calculator lets you enter a set of scores and find where a particular student's score ranks among the group.

🤖

EduSpark

EduSpark is a built-in AI assistant powered by Google Gemini. It has read-only access to your class data — students, marks, attendance, and timetable — and can answer questions, write remarks, and give suggestions based on your actual data.

To use it, you need a free Gemini API key. Go to aistudio.google.com, sign in with your Google account, and click "Create API key". Copy the key (it starts with AIza…) and paste it into the key field at the top of the AI screen. The status will change to ✓ Key set.

You can then ask anything, for example: "Which students in 8A are at risk of failing?", "Write a progress remark for my top student", or "Summarise attendance for last month". Click one of the suggestion chips to try a pre-written prompt.

If you see an "API limit reached" message, click the Clear Chat button in the top-right of the AI screen to start a fresh session and try again. The free tier of Gemini resets every minute.

⚠ Note: Your API key is stored only in your browser and is never sent anywhere other than Google's servers. Do not share your key with anyone.
💾

Backup & Data Safety

All your data is saved in your browser's local storage. It survives closing the browser and restarting the computer — but it is permanently removed if you clear your browser's site data.

To protect your data, regularly click "Download Backup" at the bottom of the sidebar. This saves a GradeSight_backup_username_date.json file containing all your credentials and app data.

If you ever lose your data (e.g. after clearing the browser or switching devices), go to the login screen and click "Restore from Backup File" in the yellow warning box. Select your backup file — your username will be filled in automatically. Enter your password and log in. All your data will be restored.

💡 Tip: Save your backup file to Google Drive so it is accessible even if your computer is replaced or reset.

About GradeSight Pro

GradeSight Pro was built out of a very real frustration — watching teachers juggle spreadsheets, paper mark sheets, and scattered Excel Sheets just to manage their classrooms. The goal from day one was simple: bring everything a Greenwood High teacher needs into a single, fast, beautiful interface that actually gets out of the way and lets them teach.

Every feature was designed around real classroom workflows. The exam tabs mirror the school's actual assessment structure — Unit Tests and Terms. The timetable builder reflects the way periods are actually planned. The countdown timer keeps teachers aware of upcoming events without having to check a separate calendar app.

Class teachers — the ones responsible for an entire section — get a special 👑 Crown Mode that unlocks marks entry across every subject for their class, not just the subjects they personally teach. The platform then does the heavy lifting: calculating totals, percentages, class averages, and surfacing the top performer automatically.
